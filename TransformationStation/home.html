What are classroom transformations?

Classrooms transformations are a great way to engage your students and to make a engaging learning environment. Transformations can be anything that will turn your classroom in a new and exciting learning environment. Some teachers have transformations that are as simple as giving their students a construction hat and an apron. While others will tape plastic table cloths on their windows, and desks. Then have inflated dinosaurs propped up all around the room. Classroom transformations can be used for one day, a week, or a month. It's totally up to the teacher and their lesson plans.

Why are classroom transformations so great?

Keeping your students engaged with fractions and compound sentences can be tough. Most students are thinking, "This is so boring". Obviously you don't want you kids to be bored. However, you have your standards that are sent by the state that you have to teach. This is where Classroom Transformations come in. Classroom transformations grab your students attention and make learning fun for them. They are so busy enjoying cutting a pizza that they don't realize that they are using the fractions that you taught them.

Where do I start?

Classroom transformations are super easy! It can be as simple as putting table cloths on their desks and giving them a magnifying glass. It can also be as extravagant as changing your entire classroom. The biggest and most important thing about transformations is spend as less as you can. Beg and borrow items whereever you can! Many businesses are willing to donate items. You can borrow from other teachers in your building. Sometimes you can even use items that are in your own home. There are times where you will spend your own money. However, if you buy durable items, you can use them again and again. Some things can be used for different transformations within one school year. Other times there are items that you will just use from year to year.

Transformations

Grocery Store

For the Grocery Store transformation our class was studying Money. To make the grocery store as real life as possible, I asked parents, neighbors, and family members to send me their cleaned trash. I used testing dividers to separate the different sections. The desks were organized to make "aisles" in the classroom. I bought some plastic fruit from the Dollar Tree for my Garden transformation and I used it for this transformation too. During this transformation, my students had a shopping list. They chose items off of their list and then they had to add up how much they spent. There were also other questions on their paper that had them count coins to determine the value.

Pizzeria

The Pizzeria transformation is a favorite amongst many! During this transformation, I play Italian Pizzeria music. The students are studying are fractions. They are tasked with making a pizza and cutting the ingredients into thirds, halves, and fourths. For this transformation I provide the students with Lunchable Pizzas. In past years, I have had parents to donate these. The room is decorated to look like an actual Pizzeria. The table cloths were purchased from Amazon. I used little candles and placemats on their desks to make them feel like they were really at a restaurant. The large pizzas on the wall were made out of butcher paper. This transformation is fun and memorable because they students get to eat their fractions after they've completed their work.

Football Game

The Football transformation is perfect for the Friday before Super Bowl Sunday. During this transformation, there wasn't a particular skill that we used. During Math we reviewed many skills with Football themed activities. ELA wasn't centered around Football at all. I just used football themed slides in my powerpoint. The kids were involved in this transformation by wearing their favorite teams jersey or sweatshirts. I was dressed as a referee. Many of the decor items were purchased from Amazon and Oriental Trading. You'll be surprised at how many different thematic items stores will have. The lights that I have in my classroom always add a magical touch. I purchased those from Amazon as well. I use them with every transformation.

Dinosaur

Aaaaah! Dinosaurs! This transformation was a huge success! Not only did we transform our classroom, but we also transformed the hallway as well. When the kids came to school that day, we had dinosaur growls playing thoughout the hallway. We covered the lights with green butcher paper to give a green jungle hue. We also had the theme music from Jurassic Park playing. We used white butcher paper to make fossils and used brown butcher paper for dino paw prints. All of this definitely grabbed their interests and had them wondering what was in store for them once they got into the classroom. I decorated my room with donated dinosaur inflatables, and ivy vines that I purchased from Amazon. I also purchased safari hats, and binoculars for the kids. I played sound affects all day long. Hearing dinosaur growls definitely kept the kids on their toes all day.

Beach Day

Beach day is one of my favorites. This was a transformation for the very last day of school and I defintely went all out. I cooked meatballs, and baked cupcakes! This was their special end of the year treat. All of the students' desks were moved into the corner of my room so that I could have as much space as possible. I covered up the desks with floor to ceiling butcher paper rolls. I borrowed the beach chairs, and kiddie pool from our PE teacher. The teacher that was in the classroom before me left tons of seashells. I used these to decorate different areas in the room. I purchased a couple of pails from the dollar tree to hold sand in. The sand came from one of our science kits that we didn't use. The other decoratoins were from my friend's swimming pool parties that she used to have. This is why it's so important to ask any and everyone you know for help. If I had bought everything that I used in this transformation, I would have spent hundreds of dollars.

Garden

The garden transformation was my very first transformation. It was super neat. I was reading a book title "Tops and Bottoms". It was a story about a bear and a rabbit growing vegetables. I purchased plastic vegetables from the Dollar Tree to use a decorations. The kids went "digging" for clues to determine the central message of the story. Another teacher on my team let me borrow her flower decor from her room. I also borrowed a shovel from my friend's dad. I dressed up as a farmer. For this to be my very first transformation, it turned out great! The lesson was a success and the kids had fun.
